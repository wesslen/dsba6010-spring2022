Motivations for Bayesian Methods

  • The Foundations of Statistics (Savage 1972)

  • Confidence intervals vs Bayesian intervals (Jaynes and Kempthorne 1976)

  • Probability theory: The logic of science (Jaynes 2003)

  • My Journey From Frequentist to Bayesian Statistics (Harrell 2021)

NHST / p-hacking

  • Statistical Evidence in Experimental Psychology: An Empirical Comparison Using 855 t Tests (Wetzels et al. 2011)

  • The garden of forking paths: Why multiple comparisons can be a problem, even when there is no “fishing expedition” or “p-hacking” and the research hypothesis was posited ahead of time (Gelman and Loken 2013)

  • The New Statistics: Why and How (Cumming 2014)

  • Fair Statistical Communication in HCI (Dragicevic 2016)

  • When Null Hypothesis Significance Testing Is Unsuitable for Research: A Reassessment (Szucs and Ioannidis 2017)

  • Abandon Statistical Significance (McShane et al. 2019)

  • Why p-values should be interpreted as p-values and not as measures of evidence (Lakens 2021)

Bayes in Practice

  • A guide to working with country-year panel data and Bayesian multilevel models (Heiss 2021)

DAGs / Causal Inference

  • Ways to close backdoors in DAGs (Heiss 2020)

Textbooks

  • Probabilistic Programming & Bayesian Methods for Hackers (Davidson-Pilon 2015)

  • Flexible imputation of missing data (Van Buuren 2018)

  • An introduction to probabilistic programming (Meent et al. 2018)

  • Think Bayes: Bayesian Statistics in Python (Downey 2021)
